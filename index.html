Who Am I?

I'm an Aerospace Engineer with a Bachelor's & Master's from the University of Maryland in the USA. I'm also a software developer. While I did't get a rigorous software engineering degree, I've studied the discipline "along the way" and have plenty of experience in aerospace-related simulations & beyond.

What Am I Doing?

My interest is in the space define by the intersection of aerospace engineering and software engineering. I like aerospace topics and I like software-driven scientific experimentation. Fortunately, there's a lot that fits into that region.

Open To Collaboration

I'm always interested in new problems to solve and new things to simulate. If you have an idea and think I can help, let me know.

Recently, I've been thinking a lot about Flight Management Systems (FMS) and how they could be used for UAV systems. I have some professional experience with FMS and suspect these kinds of systems could be re-imagined to provide a lot of strategic advantage to UAV users.

Aside from that, I enjoy control theory topics like this component library, engaging other simulation frameworks like OpenSourcePhysics, ODE propagation, and working on interesting problems in scientific software design.

Why buffetboy2001?

Sure, it's a little silly. The prefix, buffetboy, comes from a nickname I received in college. I went to buffets. A lot. Chinese ones. Yup. And the number? Well, buffetboy was too generic by itself.
